Operational Savings Stream
► Why this number?

Scotiabank currently runs AxiomSL ControllerView, internal Python pipelines, SAS 9 batch jobs, and Oracle-based reporting in parallel — each requiring separate licensing, support, and integration maintenance. SAS Viya consolidates all onto one cloud-native platform.

Peer banks consolidating 4–6 analytics tools report $3–6M annual savings (Gartner TCO study, 2023).

► Why this number?

On-premises compute for regulatory capital runs is provisioned for peak (quarter-end) and sits idle 70% of the time. SAS Viya on Azure/AWS scales elastically — Scotia can decommission on-prem batch servers and reduce infrastructure by 40–60%.

Similar cloud migrations in Canadian banking: $2–5M per year (IDC Cloud ROI Survey 2023).

► Why this number?

OSFI Basel III/IV returns (BCAR, TLAC, Pillar 3) currently require 60–100 hours of manual data assembly per quarter. SAS Visual Analytics with pre-built OSFI templates automates data-to-report in minutes.

FTE savings + reduced external audit review: $1.5–3M/yr (EY Canadian Bank Regulatory Reporting Survey 2022).

► Why this number?

Moving a new IRB model from validation to production currently takes 6–9 months at BNS. SAS Viya's MLOps pipelines reduce this to 6–8 weeks. Faster model cycles = earlier capital benefit realization.

FTE cost of slow deployment: $2–4M/yr (McKinsey Model Factory report, 2022).

► Why this number?

Model validation teams spend 40–60% of their time collecting evidence that modern platforms produce automatically (run logs, data lineage, sensitivity reports). SAS Model Manager auto-generates complete validation packages.

Industry: 10–20% reduction in validation FTE effort post-platform modernization (SR 11-7 compliance benchmarks, KPMG 2023).
